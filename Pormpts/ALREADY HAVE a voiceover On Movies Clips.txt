PHASE 2 — STRICT RULES
════════════════════════════════════════════════════

CHARACTER NAMES:
→ NEVER use real character names from the video
→ Replace every character with a neutral common name:
   Male → Jake, Ryan, Marcus, Danny, Cole
   Female → Sarah, Mia, Emma, Lily, Zoe
→ Use the SAME replacement name throughout
→ Multiple characters = different replacement names

NEVER MENTION:
→ Movie name / Show name / Documentary name
→ Any actor, director, or real person's name
→ Any franchise or brand name
→ Any channel name or creator name

DIALOGUE RULE:
→ NEVER directly quote any original spoken words
→ Convert all speech into third-person narration
→ Narrate what was MEANT, FELT, or DECIDED
   — not what was literally said
→ Wrong: He said "I will never give up"
→ Right: "He made a decision that day that 
         nothing — and no one — would stop him."

FORBIDDEN PHRASES:
→ "In this video..."
→ "In this scene..."
→ "As we can see..."
→ "The character..."
→ "So basically..."
→ "At this point..."
→ Any direct reference to the original content

PHASE 3 — HOOK OPENING LINE
════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The very first line of your script is the most 
important line you will write.

It must appear as TEXT ON SCREEN in the first 
1–2 seconds AND be the first words spoken.

HOOK RULES:
→ Maximum 12 words
→ Must create INSTANT curiosity, emotion, or tension
→ Must make the viewer feel they CANNOT scroll away
→ Written in one of these proven styles:

STORY INTRODUCTION HOOKS:
"This is the story of a [person] who [did something 
 extraordinary/shocking/heartbreaking]."
"This is what happens when [situation] 
 pushes someone beyond their limit."
"This is the moment [name] decided 
 everything was about to change."

CURIOSITY/QUESTION HOOKS:
"Would you do what [name] did 
 to protect the ones you love?"
"What would you do if everything 
 you built was taken from you overnight?"
"Could you stay silent while 
 watching this happen to someone you love?"

ACTION/TENSION HOOKS:
"Watch how one [man/woman/child] 
 changed everything with a single decision."
"Watch what happens when [name] 
 discovers the truth they were never meant to find."

MORAL/CHALLENGE HOOKS:
"Will you support what [name] did — 
 or does it go too far?"
"Was [name] a hero or a villain? 
 You decide."
"Not everyone will agree with 
 what [name] chose to do next."

CHOOSE THE HOOK STYLE that best fits 
the emotion of this specific story.

PHASE 4 — NARRATION SCRIPT
════════════════════════════════════════════════════

CRITICAL WORD COUNT RULE:
Your script must contain the EXACT same number 
of words as the original voiceover.
This is non-negotiable.
The word count you measured in Phase 1 is your 
strict target — not approximate, not close — EXACT.

If original = 187 words → your script = 187 words
If original = 312 words → your script = 312 words

SPEAKING SPEED RULE:
Match the original WPM exactly.
Your script will be recorded at the same pace.
This ensures perfect sync with the video length.

NARRATION VOICE:
→ Dramatic. Cinematic. Authoritative.
→ True crime narrator meets thriller audiobook
→ Third person always
→ Short sentences (4–8 words) during tension/action
→ Longer sentences (12–18 words) during 
  emotional or psychological moments
→ Build tension progressively — each section 
  slightly more intense than the last

PSYCHOLOGICAL DEPTH:
For every major action — add one line revealing 
the character's internal reasoning or emotion.
This is what separates great narration from summary.

Weak: "Jake ran toward the burning building."
Strong: "Jake ran toward the burning building — 
        not because he was brave. 
        Because he couldn't live with himself 
        if he didn't."

OUTPUT FORMAT FOR EVERY LINE:

[TIMESTAMP] | NARRATION LINE | (word count) | [BEAT TYPE]

BEAT TYPES:
[hook] — opening curiosity trigger
[setup] — establishing character and situation
[action] — what the character physically does
[internal] — character's thoughts, feelings, decisions
[tension] — stakes rising, danger approaching
[reveal] — truth or twist landing
[reaction] — response to what just happened
[escalation] — situation intensifying
[pivot] — the moment everything changes
[close] — final emotional beat before CTA

RUNNING WORD COUNT:
After every 5 lines add a running total:
// Running total: [X] words of [target] //

This keeps you on track to hit the exact word count.

ORIGINAL CTA — CRITICAL RULES:

→ Your CTA must be UNIQUE to this specific story
→ Must encourage viewers to share their OPINION 
  or FEELINGS in the comments

CTA FORMULAS — model yours on these:

 Drop your thoughts below."

"What would YOU have done in [name]'s position? 
 Comment below."
"Did [name] deserve what happened? 
 Let me know what you think."

CREATE A CUSTOM CTA for this specific story —
do not use a generic template, make it personal 
to the events of this exact clip.
